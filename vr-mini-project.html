UniVRsity //
VR Mini-Project


The goal of this mini-project was to spend some time familiarizing ourselves with 1-2 digital prototyping tools by working towards developing digital versions of 2-3 interactions from in-class physical prototyping.

Problem Statement

How might we use VR technology to increase access to education for college-level students?

Concept Development

→ Understanding the Problem

Explored scope in a collaborative design jam group aimed at using VR to solve for our problem statement.

→ Ideation

Compiled a list of UI elements that would feed our VR interactions:

  1. Heads Up Display (HUD)
  2. 360 Classroom
  3. Video Player
  4. 3D Objects (Desk, etc.)
  5. User Controls (start, pause, end buttons for lecture video player)
  6. Dialog Box (lecture audio, closed captioning)

→ Diverge

By sketching our environment, our group was able to come together to focus on an online learning experience that puts the user in an immersive 360 classroom so that they receive the same enriching learning experience as they would in a real life academic setting.

Physical Prototyping

To quickly bring our concept to life and flush out interactions based on UI elements, we used physical prototyping to help us understand the spatial context and application of VR. For this stage in our process, we used a range of mixed media, from paper to PlayDoh.

Digital Prototyping

We explored various tools and settled on A-Frame for digital prototyping based on its scalability.

The intended scenario is that the user would hover their gaze over the start button to start the lecture video. For accessibility, text would populate for the closed captioning feature accompanied by audio.

We expanded on in-class interactions by implementing closed captioning and gaze within a 360 classroom.
